Matthew Chatfield BSc CEnv CIEEM

Personal profile

I am a confident manager, team leader, problem-solver and communicator with a record of success across a range of projects. I have in-depth experience of landscape, ecology, heritage and visitor management, with knowledge of planning, budget control, lottery funding, interpretation and practical land management. An active writer and commentator, I also work as a social media and web design professional with an up-to-date knowledge of online and print marketing and production.

Key skills

  • I’ve got plenty of experience of using the media and the internet to get my messages out. I have good working relationships with local media, both online and traditional.
  • I’m an enthusiastic communicator with knowledge of and passion for my home county, and I regularly lead guided walks around the Isle of Wight’s beautiful countryside.
  • I manage and develop complex and multi-functional, predominantly outdoor, sites; balancing conflicting constraints and demands.
  • I’m a very experienced team leader, used to managing a diverse team and getting the best out of my staff and volunteers.
  • Working with visitors and the public for most of my career, particularly in outdoor and countryside sites, I’m used to stressful situations and am very good at satisfying complaints and solving problems.
  • I am not afraid to get my hands dirty, and still take the opportunity to use my skills in litter picking, car park marshalling and covering reception.
  • I am used to handling large budgets and competitively procuring and supervising contracts and supplies. I’ve been involved in many grant applications and successful grant-funded projects.
  • I have an abiding interest in computers and internet technologies, including website design and deployment, practical IT support and purchase skills, as well as up-to-date understanding of social media.
