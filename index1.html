Matthew Chatfield is the creator and editor of Naturenet, the internet's online resource for practical nature conservation and countryside management. See Matthew's full CV.

Not only is Matthew the editor of Naturenet, he is employed as a Communications Officer by the Isle of Wight Council.

Born in Sussex, 1967, to a clergy family, his arrival was heralded by a cartoon in the local paper suggesting that his mother, Janet, was about to bear three other sons named Mark, Luke and John. This did not occur, for which he was heartily grateful and no doubt so was she.

His father, Norman, was a Canon Residentiary at Gloucester Cathedral until 2002, when he retired. No, he wasn't fired. Matthew was brought up, indeed, as the son of a gun.

Along with his two sisters Bec and Liz his childhood was spent on the Isle of Wight and in various parts of South Hampshire. Living in sundry vicarages for most of that time he became adept at dealing with questions such as "Are you going to be a monk when you grow up?"; "Is your dad a Catholic or Protestant?" (think about that one for a bit); and "Don't you sound like your father?"
